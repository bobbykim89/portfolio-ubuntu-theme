### Purdue University

#### Bachelors of science in industrial engineering

May 2018

<br/>

My coding journey started out rough. In college, most of my exposure was MATLAB, which mostly just meant waiting around for a seat in a crowded computer lab. Not exactly a great origin story.

<br/>

Things started to click when a physics course had me writing Python scripts to simulate moving objects. What hooked me wasn't the physics, it was the fact that Python ran on my own laptop. Small thing, big deal.

<br/>

The real turning point was a team project where we built a Monte Carlo simulation and a mock company website around it. I took on the backend role with basically zero idea what that meant, and spent the rest of the semester Googling my way through ER diagrams, SQL queries, and security practices. It was a grind, but something about building a system that actually worked got me hooked. That project is what made me want to do this for real.
